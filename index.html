Research

M.Tech Thesis: Variance-Adaptive Scaling for Transformer Attention Mechanisms

IIT Patna | 2024 - Present | Potential Future Publication

Abstract

Transformer-based models have achieved state-of-the-art performance across diverse natural language processing tasks. However, their attention mechanism exhibits a critical and underexplored failure mode in short-text classification‚Äîattention collapse, wherein attention weights converge to uniform distributions during training, resulting in semantic information loss and severe accuracy degradation.

This phenomenon occurs when input feature variance falls below critical thresholds, destabilizing the softmax dynamics of the scaled dot-product attention. While extensively deployed in resource-constrained environments (mobile, IoT, edge devices), existing short-text models suffer from undiagnosed collapse, reducing efficiency and reliability without actionable mitigation strategies.

Methodology & Contribution

This thesis advances a unified diagnostic and mitigation paradigm rooted in optimization and signal stability theory. We first delineate collapse through entropy profiling across benchmarks, uncovering a direct covariance between input feature depletion and attention homogenization. Extending Bishop's variational framework for neural network stability to multi-head dynamics, we derive rigorous stability bounds that reveal how standard scaling worsens fragility under low-variance conditions.

Motivated by these insights, we introduce Variance-Adaptive Scaling (VAS): a lightweight, parameter-free adjustment that calibrates the attention temperature via batch-wise variance estimates, enforcing robust softmax equilibria without architectural overhead.

Results & Impact

Empirical scrutiny on a compact 4-layer Transformer (256 hidden dimensions) affirms VAS's efficacy:

  • ‚úì Attention entropy surged from 0.15 to 0.68‚Äîa 377% restoration
  • ‚úì SST-2 accuracy climbed 18.3% by epoch 5
  • ‚úì Convergence hastened 43% via smoother loss landscapes (verified through Hessian-free approximations)
  • ‚úì On RTX 3050: <1 MB footprint and trivial latency

Beyond rectification, our toolkit equips practitioners with entropy diagnostics for proactive collapse detection, fostering interpretable, resilient Transformers in production short-text pipelines. This research demonstrates viability for battery-constrained inference in edge computing environments.
